Here we come to the heart of the matter: Ive never left Istanbul – never left the houses, streets and neighbourhoods of my childhood. Although Ive lived in other districts from time to time, fifty years on I find myself back in the Pamuk Apartments, where my first photographs were taken and where my mother first held me in her arms to show me the world. I know this persistence owes something to my imaginary friend, and to the solace I took from the bond between us. But we live in an age defined by mass migration and creative immigrants, and so I am sometimes hard-pressed to explain why Ive stayed not only in the same place, but the same building.
